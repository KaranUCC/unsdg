Goal 15: Life On Land

Protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, and halt and reverse land degradation and halt biodiversity loss

What is Goal 15?

Goal 15 (Life on Land) is a goal aimed at preserving the precious ecosystems, forests, and biodiversity that exist on our planet. It's a response to the challenges we face, like deforestation, habitat loss, and the decline of wildlife. The targets of Goal 15 include conserving forests and biodiversity, promoting sustainable land management practices, fighting against wildlife trafficking, ensuring fair sharing of resources, and creating global cooperation for sustainable development.

Why is Goal 15 Important?

It is vital for protecting nature, preserving biodiversity, and ensuring a sustainable future. By safeguarding forests, ecosystems, and wildlife, we maintain a healthy planet with clean air, water, and fertile soil. Goal 15 tackles deforestation, climate change, and habitat loss, promoting sustainable practices that benefit both nature and humans. To create a sustainable future for the next generations to come, the targets of Goal 15 must be met.
