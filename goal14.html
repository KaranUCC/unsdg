Goal 14: Life Below Water

Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development

What is Goal 14?

Goal 14 (Life Below Water) is a goal aimed at protecting and responsibly using our oceans, seas, and marine resources. This goal recognizes the challenges we face including pollution, overfishing, habitat destruction, and climate change. To address these issues, the targets of Goal 14 focus on conserving marine ecosystems, promoting sustainable fisheries management, reducing marine pollution, preserving marine biodiversity, and supporting small-scale fisheries. Goal 14 aims to ensure the long-term health of our oceans for the benefit of current and future generations of humans and marine animals.

Why is Goal 14 Important?

It is crucial because oceans provide livelihoods for millions, regulate the Earth's climate, and host incredible biodiversity. Protecting and managing marine ecosystems ensures food security, supports sustainable economic development, and mitigates climate change impacts
